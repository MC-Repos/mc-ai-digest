Investigating the Viability of Employing Multi-modal Large Language Models in the Context of Audio Deepfake Detection

💼 35 ⚡ 78

This paper investigates whether Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) like Qwen2-Audio-7B-Instruct and SALMONN can detect audio deepfakes by combining audio inputs with crafted text prompts for feature-guided reasoning, testing both zero-shot and fine-tuned evaluation modes. The approach leverages cross-modal understanding to enable robust feature learning for audio authenticity detection, addressing a gap where MLLMs have succeeded in image/video deepfake detection but remain largely unexplored for audio. The work suggests that context-rich, question-answer prompts may facilitate deeper multimodal understanding, offering a potential alternative to traditional audio-specific deepfake detection architectures.

JavisGPT: A Unified Multi-modal LLM for Sounding-Video Comprehension and Generation

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-01-05

💼 22 ⚡ 87

JavisGPT introduces the first unified MLLM for joint audio-video comprehension and generation through an encoder-LLM-decoder architecture with a novel SyncFusion module that performs spatio-temporal audio-video fusion and maintains temporal coherence. The model leverages a three-stage training pipeline (pretraining, fine-tuning, instruction-tuning) and a 200K+ curated instruction dataset (JavisInst-Omni) to enable multimodal understanding and synchronized audio-video generation from natural language instructions. This represents a significant step toward end-to-end multimodal AI systems that can understand and generate temporally coherent audiovisual content, with practical applications in video synthesis, accessibility, and creative content generation.

Latent Flow Matching for Expressive Singing Voice Synthesis

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-01-05

💼 23 ⚡ 78

FM-Singer addresses the prior-posterior mismatch problem in cVAE-based singing voice synthesis by introducing conditional flow matching in latent space, which learns a continuous vector field that transports prior samples toward posterior latents via optimal-transport-inspired paths. At inference, an ODE solver refines prior latents before waveform generation, enabling fine-grained expressiveness improvements (vibrato, micro-prosody) without sacrificing the parallel decoding efficiency of VAE-based systems. This approach elegantly bridges the train-test distribution gap that typically degrades synthesis quality in conditional generative models for audio.

This arXiv paper argues that GenAI's core threat isn't isolated deepfakes but the systemic erosion of trust infrastructure through synthetic realities—coherent, interactive environments where content, identity, and interaction are jointly generated and self-reinforcing, making verification at scale technically infeasible. The authors reframe the problem as a socio-technical shift requiring examination of institutional practices rather than treating GenAI harms as incremental extensions of traditional misinformation. This suggests downstream implications for cryptographic verification, provenance systems, and architectural approaches to maintaining epistemic integrity in GenAI-saturated information ecosystems.

MotionPhysics: Learnable Motion Distillation for Text-Guided Simulation

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-01-05

💼 32 ⚡ 35

MotionPhysics introduces a text-guided physics simulation framework that leverages multimodal LLMs for material parameter estimation and distills motion priors from pretrained video diffusion models to infer realistic physical behavior without ground-truth trajectories. The key innovation is the learnable motion distillation loss that extracts dynamic motion semantics from generative models while decoupling appearance and geometry, enabling end-to-end differentiable parameter optimization from natural language alone. This approach significantly reduces expert tuning overhead for 3D simulation, potentially democratizing physics-based animation for non-specialists while bridging the gap between generative AI models and traditional physics engines.

TeleWorld: Towards Dynamic Multimodal Synthesis with a 4D World Model

💼 23 ⚡ 72

TeleWorld presents a 4D world modeling framework that addresses critical limitations in current video generation by introducing a closed-loop generation-reconstruction-guidance paradigm—where generated video streams are continuously reconstructed into dynamic spatio-temporal representations that feed back to guide subsequent generation, ensuring spatial-temporal-physical consistency. This approach enables real-time multimodal synthesis with long-horizon coherence and persistent scene memory, moving beyond static video generation toward interactive, practical world models suitable for embodied AI systems. The key innovation lies in treating world modeling as a bidirectional process rather than forward-only generation, enabling both improved consistency and the potential for real-time user interaction within coherent virtual environments.

HyperPriv-EPN: Hypergraph Learning with Privileged Knowledge for Ependymoma Prognosis

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-01-05

💼 15 ⚡ 25

HyperPriv-EPN introduces a novel hypergraph-based Learning Using Privileged Information (LUPI) framework that leverages post-operative surgical reports as privileged knowledge to improve preoperative ependymoma prognosis from MRI alone. The key innovation is a "Severed Graph Strategy" using dual-stream distillation where a Teacher graph enriched with surgical data guides a Student graph to learn semantic community structures from visual features, enabling knowledge transfer without requiring text data at inference time. Validated on 311 multi-center patients, this approach achieves SOTA diagnostic accuracy and survival stratification—demonstrating practical value for clinical deployment where privileged surgical information is unavailable during real-time prognosis.
