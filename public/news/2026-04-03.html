💼 32 ⚡ 72

TempoControl enables fine-grained temporal control in text-to-video generation by optimizing cross-attention maps during inference without retraining, using three principles: correlation (aligning temporal patterns with control signals), magnitude (adjusting attention strength), and entropy (preserving semantic consistency). This approach allows users to precisely specify when visual concepts appear in generated videos while maintaining quality, addressing a critical limitation in current diffusion-based video models. The method's inference-only design makes it practical for deployment across existing pre-trained models.

This approach has significant implications for unified AI systems, potentially reducing engineering complexity of external decoder orchestration while enabling more coherent cross-modal reasoning.

Learning Humanoid Navigation from Human Data

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-04-03

💼 25 ⚡ 42

EgoNav demonstrates a novel approach to humanoid robot navigation by training a diffusion model exclusively on 5 hours of human egocentric walking data, eliminating the need for robot-specific training data or domain adaptation. The system leverages multi-modal visual conditioning (color, depth, semantics, and frozen DINOv3 features) combined with trajectory history to predict distributions of plausible future paths, enabling real-time inference through a hybrid sampling scheme and receding-horizon planning. First Logit Boosting: Visual Grounding Method to Mitigate Object Hallucination in Large Vision-Language Models

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-04-03

💼 23 ⚡ 72

First Logit Boosting (FLB) addresses object hallucination in Large Vision-Language Models through a training-free mechanism that mitigates "long-term decay"—where visual grounding weakens as generation progresses and language priors dominate. By storing and boosting the logit of the first generated token, FLB achieves visual grounding improvements without additional training data or external models, offering a computationally efficient alternative to existing approaches like Contrastive Decoding. This technique is particularly valuable for practitioners seeking to reduce hallucinations in production LVLMs while maintaining inference efficiency.
