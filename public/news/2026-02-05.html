💼 18 ⚡ 45

AGMA introduces a two-stage prior learning framework that adaptively constructs scene-aware Gaussian mixture priors from training data, theoretically proving that prediction error is fundamentally lower-bounded by prior quality—shifting focus from model architecture to prior modeling as the key performance bottleneck in multimodal trajectory forecasting. The approach extracts diverse behavioral patterns and distills them into global priors for inference, achieving SOTA results on standard benchmarks (ETH-UCY, Stanford Drone, JRDB) by better capturing the full distribution of plausible pedestrian futures compared to fixed or misaligned priors. This work has implications for improving both prediction accuracy and mode coverage in downstream applications like autonomous vehicles and crowd simulation, where prior misalignment has historically limited diversity in generated trajectories.

💼 28 ⚡ 72

KVSmooth introduces a training-free, plug-and-play technique that reduces hallucinations in MLLMs by applying entropy-guided adaptive exponential moving average smoothing to KV-Cache keys and values, dynamically adjusting based on token attention entropy to combat semantic drift during decoding. This approach is particularly valuable because it requires no retraining and directly addresses the core issue of visual grounding inconsistency as sequence length increases—a critical bottleneck for reliable MLLM deployment in production environments.
