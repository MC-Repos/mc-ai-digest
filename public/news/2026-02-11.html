This approach enables precise emotion manipulation beyond current methods by explicitly modeling facial muscle movements as an intermediate representation, with practical applications for virtual avatars and content creation.

💼 28 ⚡ 82

This paper presents an edge-optimized multimodal emotion recognition system that fuses speech, text, and facial modalities using Dempster-Shafer theory and Dirichlet parameterization to explicitly model predictive uncertainty—a theoretically grounded alternative to standard concatenation-based fusion that operates directly on logits without requiring joint training. Quantifying Multimodal Imbalance: A GMM-Guided Adaptive Loss for Audio-Visual Learning

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-11

💼 28 ⚡ 85

This paper addresses a critical problem in audio-visual learning by introducing a quantitative, sample-level solution to modality imbalance—where dominant modalities suppress weaker ones during training. The key innovation is using a GMM to model the bimodal distribution of prediction discrepancies (Modality Gap metric), enabling dynamic, Bayesian-informed reweighting rather than static heuristics that ignore outlier samples with poor data quality. This approach has significant implications for practitioners, potentially improving multimodal fusion robustness by adaptively handling the heterogeneous nature of sample contributions rather than applying uniform modulation strategies.
