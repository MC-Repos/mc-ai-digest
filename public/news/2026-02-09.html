EgoAVU: Egocentric Audio-Visual Understanding

💼 25 ⚡ 78

EgoAVU addresses a critical gap in multimodal AI by introducing a scalable data engine that automatically generates coherent audio-visual narrations and QA pairs for egocentric videos through cross-modal correlation modeling, overcoming the scarcity of joint-modality labeled data that limits current MLLMs. The framework combines token-based video filtering and graph-based curation to ensure dataset quality and diversity, resulting in a 3M-sample training dataset (EgoAVU-Instruct) and manually-verified benchmark, enabling proper evaluation of audio-visual understanding in embodied AI scenarios.

An item is worth one token in Multimodal Large Language Models-based Sequential Recommendation

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-09

💼 22 ⚡ 72

This paper proposes Speeder, a multimodal LLM-based sequential recommendation system that addresses three critical bottlenecks: inefficient item tokenization (replacing lengthy descriptions with single-token representations), modality bias in predominantly text-trained LLMs, and attention degradation over long interaction sequences. The key innovation appears to be compressing item representations to one token per item while maintaining multimodal information (images, relationships), improving both computational efficiency and the model's ability to capture long-range sequential dependencies. For practitioners, this approach offers significant inference speedups and better handling of rich item metadata without expanding context length—critical for deploying MLLMs in real-world recommendation systems.

FlashBlock: Attention Caching for Efficient Long-Context Block Diffusion

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-09

💼 28 ⚡ 72

FlashBlock introduces cached block-external attention to optimize long-context diffusion models by exploiting cross-step redundancy—attention outputs from tokens outside the current block remain stable across diffusion steps, enabling reuse without recomputation. This approach reduces both attention computation and KV cache memory access overhead while maintaining the efficiency gains of block-wise causal inference used in diffusion language models and video generation. The technique addresses a critical bottleneck in scaling diffusion to minute-long videos and extended texts by avoiding redundant attention calculations without architectural modifications.

ForeHOI: Feed-forward 3D Object Reconstruction from Daily Hand-Object Interaction Videos

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-09

💼 28 ⚡ 35

ForeHOI presents a feed-forward neural model for reconstructing 3D object geometry from monocular hand-object interaction videos in under one minute, addressing the severe occlusion challenge through joint 2D mask inpainting and 3D shape completion. The key innovation is coupling these two tasks within a single feed-forward framework, which outperforms traditional optimization-based approaches while eliminating preprocessing requirements. This approach has significant implications for embodied AI systems that need real-time 3D scene understanding from egocentric video without expensive per-scene optimization.

CompEvent: Complex-valued Event-RGB Fusion for Low-light Video Enhancement and Deblurring

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-09

💼 18 ⚡ 78

**CompEvent** introduces a complex-valued neural network that fuses event camera data with RGB frames for low-light video enhancement, moving beyond staged fusion approaches through a Complex Temporal Alignment GRU that iteratively processes both modalities and a Complex Space-Frequency Learning module operating in spatial-frequency domains. This holistic fusion strategy addresses the fundamental challenge of simultaneous low-light sensitivity and motion deblurring degradations common in surveillance and autonomous driving by leveraging event cameras' superior temporal resolution and inherent low-light performance. The complex-valued formulation enables more sophisticated signal representations during joint restoration, suggesting improved handling of the intertwined temporal and spatial artifacts typical in extreme low-light scenarios.

This technique offers practical advantages for high-fidelity video dubbing by maintaining facial structure coherence and dynamic motion realism while achieving precise synchronization.

FlashBlock: Attention Caching for Efficient Long-Context Block Diffusion

💼 25 ⚡ 75

FlashBlock exploits cross-step attention redundancy in block diffusion by caching stable out-of-block attention computations across diffusion steps, reducing both attention computation and KV cache I/O overhead for long-context generation. This approach maintains generation quality while significantly improving inference efficiency for extended video and text synthesis without requiring architectural modifications. The key insight—that external block attention converges quickly while internal attention changes substantially—enables a practical optimization layer for existing diffusion-based language models and video generators.

💼 28 ⚡ 42

This approach, validated on a new 27K+ instance dataset, has direct implications for embodied AI, 3D scene understanding, and spatial reasoning tasks requiring viewpoint-agnostic scene representations.

STACodec: Semantic Token Assignment for Balancing Acoustic Fidelity and Semantic Information in Audio Codecs

eess.AS updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-09

💼 23 ⚡ 87

STACodec addresses the trade-off between acoustic fidelity and semantic richness in neural audio codecs by embedding semantic information directly into the first RVQ layer via semantic token assignment, rather than through lossy distillation. The proposed semantic pre-distillation module eliminates dependency on external SSL-based tokenizers during inference, improving efficiency while maintaining superior reconstruction quality compared to existing hybrid approaches. This design enables neural codecs to serve dual roles—high-fidelity audio compression and token-based language model integration—without sacrificing either capability.
