MOVA: Towards Scalable and Synchronized Video-Audio Generation

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-10

💼 25 ⚡ 87

MOVA introduces the first open-source joint video-audio generation model using a scalable 32B-parameter Mixture-of-Experts architecture that simultaneously produces synchronized video and audio (including lip-synced speech, sound effects, and music), addressing critical limitations of cascaded pipelines that accumulate errors and increase computational cost. The IT2VA (Image-Text to Video-Audio) framework enables multimodal generation in a single forward pass, advancing beyond closed-source systems like Sora 2 by providing transparency and accessibility to the research community for tackling audio-visual synchronization challenges at scale.

MS-Mix: Unveiling the Power of Mixup for Multimodal Sentiment Analysis

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-10

💼 22 ⚡ 85

MS-Mix introduces an emotion-aware data augmentation framework that addresses a critical limitation of standard Mixup approaches in multimodal sentiment analysis by intelligently selecting compatible samples to mix, preventing label ambiguity when combining text, audio, and video. The proposed Sentiment-Aware Sample Selection (SASS) strategy and adaptive mixing mechanism tackle the scarce-data problem inherent in multimodal datasets while maintaining semantic consistency across heterogeneous modalities. This approach has significant implications for improving generalization in MSA tasks where training data is limited, offering a practical solution to a well-known challenge in multimodal learning.

Task-Conditioned Probing Reveals Brain-Alignment Patterns in Instruction-Tuned Multimodal LLMs

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-10

💼 15 ⚡ 35

This study demonstrates that instruction-tuned multimodal LLMs exhibit task-dependent brain alignment patterns when predicting fMRI activity during naturalistic audiovisual stimulation, suggesting these models organize representations around functional task demands rather than just semantic surface features. The researchers use voxel-wise encoding analysis across eight different IT-MLLMs and thirteen video instructions to probe whether instruction-tuning genuinely drives functional organization aligned with human neural processing. This finding has implications for understanding how prompt-conditioning shapes internal representations in multimodal models and validates instruction-tuning as a mechanism for achieving neurobiologically-plausible task-specific encoding.

Exploring Physical Intelligence Emergence via Omni-Modal Architecture and Physical Data Engine

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-10

💼 18 ⚡ 78

OmniFysics addresses the challenge of brittle physical reasoning in multimodal models by combining an omni-modal architecture (images, audio, video, text) with a novel physical data engine that explicitly injects physics knowledge through hierarchical retrieval-based supervision and audio-visual consistency filtering. The approach uses two key components—FysicsAny for generating physics-grounded image-instruction pairs via verified prototype databases and physics-law constraints, and FysicsOmniCap for distilling high-fidelity video-instruction pairs from web data. This work is significant for robotics, simulation, and embodied AI applications where visual ambiguity traditionally undermines physical understanding, offering a scalable method to leverage structured physical priors in foundation models.

The key innovation is Oracle-Guided Bidirectional Distillation, which mitigates error accumulation and identity drift in long-sequence autoregressive generation, enabling real-time infinite-length streaming while maintaining high visual fidelity—a significant practical advancement for live video synthesis applications.

VGAS: Value-Guided Action-Chunk Selection for Few-Shot Vision-Language-Action Adaptation

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-10

💼 18 ⚡ 72

VGAS addresses few-shot VLA adaptation by decoupling generation from selection: a fine-tuned VLA generates multiple action-chunk candidates while a specialized Q-Chunk-Former critic uses geometric grounding to select trajectories that are both semantically correct and geometrically precise, resolving ambiguities that cause failures under limited supervision. This inference-time best-of-N selection approach leverages learned value functions to overcome the common failure mode where semantically plausible but geometrically imprecise actions diverge during execution with scarce demonstrations.

Do MLLMs Really See It: Reinforcing Visual Attention in Multimodal LLMs

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-10

💼 18 ⚡ 78

This paper identifies a critical weakness in MLLMs: early visual misalignment persists throughout reasoning chains without correction, causing cascading errors. The authors propose SAYO, an RL-based framework that uses region-level visual attention rewards to explicitly supervise attention dynamics, enabling models to learn stable visual grounding rather than relying on post-hoc textual reasoning alone. This approach represents a significant shift from CoT-centric improvements toward mechanistic attention control, with practical implications for improving reliability in vision-language tasks where visual grounding actually matters.

MTS-CSNet: Multiscale Tensor Factorization for Deep Compressive Sensing on RGB Images

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-10

💼 15 ⚡ 72

MTS-CSNet introduces Multiscale Tensor Summation (MTS) factorization as a learnable compressive sensing operator that overcomes receptive field limitations of convolutional approaches by performing mode-wise linear transformations across tensor dimensions, enabling efficient modeling of cross-dimensional correlations in RGB image reconstruction. The framework achieves direct tensor-space dimensionality reduction without iterative optimization, combining the MTS operator as both the sampling mechanism and reconstruction refinement stage in a parameter-efficient feed-forward architecture. This structured approach addresses scalability challenges in deep CS methods while maintaining computational efficiency for high-dimensional image data.

Reasoning-Augmented Representations for Multimodal Retrieval

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-10

💼 28 ⚡ 72

This paper addresses brittleness in universal multimodal retrieval by decoupling reasoning from embedding compression—using VLMs to densely caption images, disambiguate queries, and rewrite instructions *before* retrieval rather than forcing a single embedding to handle both reasoning and matching simultaneously. The key insight is that "silent" visual evidence and implicit query semantics force spurious feature alignment, which a data-centric approach mitigates by making latent semantics explicit through VLM-augmented representations. This requires retraining retrievers on semantically enriched data, suggesting that retrieval robustness for complex reasoning tasks demands explicit intermediate representations rather than end-to-end embedding optimization.

Action-to-Action Flow Matching

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-10

💼 23 ⚡ 35

Action-to-Action Flow Matching (A2A) replaces random Gaussian noise initialization in diffusion-based robotic policies with learned latent representations of historical action sequences, eliminating the need for multiple iterative denoising steps during inference. By embedding proprioceptive feedback as informed starting points rather than static conditioning, A2A significantly reduces inference latency while better capturing robot dynamics—a critical advancement for real-time control in robotics. This shift from denoising-centric to flow-matching approaches represents a paradigm change in how generative models can be adapted for low-latency sequential decision-making tasks.
