HiMu: Hierarchical Multimodal Frame Selection for Long Video Question Answering

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-03-20

💼 28 ⚡ 78

HiMu introduces a training-free hierarchical frame selection framework for long-form video QA that decomposes queries into a logic tree of atomic predicates, each processed by lightweight modality-specific experts (CLIP, detection, OCR, ASR, CLAP) rather than collapsing into dense vectors or requiring expensive iterative LVLM inference. The approach normalizes and temporally smoothes multimodal signals, then composes them through fuzzy-logic operators to preserve compositional structure and cross-modal bindings while staying within LVLMs' context windows. This addresses a key bottleneck in video understanding by achieving the reasoning precision of agent-based methods at near similarity-search speeds.

DEAF: A Benchmark for Diagnostic Evaluation of Acoustic Faithfulness in Audio Language Models

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-03-20

💼 23 ⚡ 78

DEAF introduces a benchmark with 2,700+ conflict stimuli to test whether Audio MLLMs genuinely process acoustic information or exploit text-based shortcuts across three dimensions (prosody, background sounds, speaker identity). The framework uses progressive multi-level evaluation with semantic conflicts and misleading prompts to isolate text bias from acoustic faithfulness, revealing that current Audio MLLMs exhibit consistent text dominance over actual acoustic signal processing. This diagnostic approach provides quantitative metrics to assess model reliance on textual cues, exposing a critical gap between reported benchmark performance and true multimodal understanding.

The framework targets practical deployment scenarios where processing long-sequence multimodal data (audio, video, text) demands both low latency and robust fusion of heterogeneous information.

# Audio-Video Generation via Cross-Modal Context Learning Researchers identify fundamental limitations in current dual-stream transformer architectures for joint audio-video generation—including manifold instability from gating mechanisms, cross-modal attention biases, and classifier-free guidance inconsistencies—and propose Cross-Modal Context Learning (CCL) to address these issues through specially designed modules. This work leverages pre-trained diffusion models while tackling the synchronization and multi-modal conditioning challenges that plague existing methods, enabling higher-quality generation with minimal training data. The approach directly targets the architectural bottlenecks preventing seamless audio-video correlation, which has significant implications for applications requiring temporal coherence across modalities.

Thinking with Constructions: A Benchmark and Policy Optimization for Visual-Text Interleaved Geometric Reasoning

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-03-20

💼 23 ⚡ 72

This work addresses a critical limitation in MLLMs for geometry: they lack the ability to dynamically construct visual aids during reasoning, unlike human problem-solvers who iteratively manipulate diagrams. The authors introduce GeoAux-Bench (4,334 problems with aligned text-visual construction steps) and demonstrate that interleaved visual-text reasoning substantially outperforms static approaches, with valid constructions acting as entropy reducers that improve reasoning coherence. This represents a meaningful shift from passive diagram interpretation toward active geometric construction planning, with implications for how MLLMs should integrate spatial reasoning into their inference process.

Motion-o: Trajectory-Grounded Video Reasoning

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-03-20

💼 28 ⚡ 72

Motion-o introduces explicit trajectory reasoning to video understanding by formalizing Spatial-Temporal-Trajectory (STT) reasoning, moving beyond implicit motion patterns in current spatio-temporal models to make object motion paths verifiable and interpretable. The approach extends visual language models with motion-centric capabilities and provides a trajectory-grounding dataset that augments sparse keyframe annotations, addressing a gap in current video reasoning benchmarks that lack structured motion pattern articulation. This has significant implications for applications requiring precise motion analysis—from autonomous systems to sports analytics—by enabling models to explicitly reason about *how* objects move rather than just what they are and where they appear.
