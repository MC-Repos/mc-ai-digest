PrismVAU introduces a two-stage VAU system that combines lightweight textual anchor-based anomaly scoring with MLLM-driven contextual refinement, eliminating expensive fine-tuning and external modules while maintaining real-time performance. cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-01-08

💼 32 ⚡ 78

SoulX-FlashTalk addresses real-time audio-driven avatar generation by introducing Self-Correcting Bidirectional Distillation, which maintains bidirectional attention within video chunks rather than enforcing strictly unidirectional mechanisms, thereby preserving spatiotemporal correlations and improving motion coherence. The 14B-parameter framework incorporates a Multi-step Retrospective Self-Correction Mechanism to stabilize infinite-duration generation while meeting strict latency constraints, offering a practical solution to the computational load vs. real-time performance tradeoff that plagues existing diffusion-based approaches.

Analyzing Reasoning Consistency in Large Multimodal Models under Cross-Modal Conflicts

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-01-08

💼 24 ⚡ 78

This paper identifies **textual inertia** as a critical failure mode in Large Multimodal Models where erroneous text in reasoning chains causes models to ignore conflicting visual evidence, systematically evaluated via the LogicGraph Perturbation Protocol across diverse LMM architectures. The study reveals poor self-correction rates (<10%) and proposes Active Visual-Context Refinement, a training-free inference method to mitigate blind error propagation in multimodal reasoning chains. The findings highlight fundamental robustness gaps in CoT reasoning for video understanding and suggest that cross-modal consistency verification is essential for production-grade LMMs.

V-Agent: An Interactive Video Search System Using Vision-Language Models

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-01-08

💼 28 ⚡ 82

V-Agent introduces a multi-agent architecture combining fine-tuned vision-language models (VLMs) with multimodal embeddings to enable context-aware video retrieval that jointly processes visual frames and ASR-transcribed audio in a shared representation space, overcoming traditional text-only retrieval limitations. The system's three-agent design (routing, search, and chat agents) enables interactive refinement of search results and user dialogue, with the core innovation being VLM fine-tuning on preference data paired with retrieval vector enhancement for more semantically accurate video-to-query matching. This approach has immediate applications for multimodal information retrieval systems that must balance visual understanding, speech comprehension, and conversational UX in real-world video search scenarios.

Rationale-Grounded In-Context Learning for Time Series Reasoning with Multimodal Large Language Models

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-01-08

💼 23 ⚡ 72

This work addresses a critical limitation in multimodal LLMs for time series: the lack of causal reasoning bridges between observations and outcomes, which causes models to learn spurious correlations rather than principled logic. RationaleTS solves this by pre-computing label-conditioned reasoning paths (rationales) and using hybrid retrieval to inject relevant causal explanations into in-context learning, enabling more robust few-shot generalization across temporal domains. The approach represents a meaningful step toward interpretable, reasoning-grounded time series forecasting with foundation models, particularly relevant for domains where explainability and transfer learning are critical.

This approach balances multimodal reasoning capabilities with efficiency constraints, making it particularly relevant for resource-constrained creative workflow systems.

💼 22 ⚡ 78

TA-Prompting addresses a critical limitation in VideoLLMs for dense video captioning by introducing **Temporal Anchors**—learnable mechanisms that precisely localize event boundaries in untrimmed videos, enabling better temporal grounding of generated captions. The method incorporates an **event coherent sampling strategy** during inference to select high-quality captions that maintain temporal consistency across multiple events and cross-modal alignment with video content. This approach bridges the gap between LLM reasoning capabilities and fine-grained temporal localization, improving both accuracy and coherence in dense video understanding tasks.

This approach has significant implications for scaling multimodal LLMs to audio, offering a principled alternative to dense adapters that struggle with the intrinsic heterogeneity of acoustic information.

SyncLipMAE: Contrastive Masked Pretraining for Audio-Visual Talking-Face Representation

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-01-08

💼 18 ⚡ 82

SyncLipMAE introduces a self-supervised framework that disentangles talking-face representation into three explicit components—identity, speech-synchronized facial dynamics (vocal motion), and audio-agnostic movements—using masked visual modeling coupled with cross-modal contrastive alignment. The key innovation is leveraging time-aligned audio-visual synchronization at the token level to drive both modalities into a shared embedding space, enabling robust transfer learning without labeled data. This approach addresses a critical challenge in audio-visual processing by decomposing entangled facial dynamics, making the learned representations more generalizable across diverse downstream tasks in talking-face understanding and generation.
