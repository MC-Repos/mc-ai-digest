DCER: Dual-Stage Compression and Energy-Based Reconstruction

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-06

💼 18 ⚡ 78

DCER introduces a dual-stage compression framework for robust multimodal fusion: within-modality frequency transforms (wavelet/DCT) denoise individual streams while cross-modality bottleneck tokens enforce true fusion, and energy-based models reconstruct missing modalities via learnable energy functions that simultaneously provide uncertainty quantification (ρ > 0.72 correlation with error). The approach achieves SOTA on sentiment analysis benchmarks while maintaining robustness to both noisy inputs and missing modalities—a critical advance for real-world multimodal systems where modality availability and quality are unpredictable.

FlashBlock: Attention Caching for Efficient Long-Context Block Diffusion

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-06

💼 32 ⚡ 78

FlashBlock addresses computational bottlenecks in long-context block diffusion by exploiting cross-step attention redundancy—specifically, that attention outputs from tokens outside the current block remain stable across diffusion steps while internal attention varies. The proposed cached block-external attention mechanism reuses these stable outputs to reduce both attention computation and KV cache accesses, offering significant efficiency gains for long-form video and text generation without architectural modifications. This work is particularly relevant for scaling diffusion models to minute-long videos and extended sequences where KV cache overhead becomes a critical bottleneck.

This shifts video generation evaluation from surface-level aesthetics toward measuring true cognitive reasoning capabilities—particularly relevant as video models increasingly need to demonstrate physical plausibility and logical coherence for real-world applications.
