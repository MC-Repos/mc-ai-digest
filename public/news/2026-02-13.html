MAPLE: Modality-Aware Post-training and Learning Ecosystem

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-13

💼 25 ⚡ 78

MAPLE addresses a critical inefficiency in multimodal LLM post-training by introducing modality-aware RL optimization that stratifies training batches based on actual task requirements rather than treating all modalities equally—reducing policy-gradient variance and improving convergence on heterogeneous inputs. The framework comprises three components: MAPLE-bench (a benchmark annotating minimal required signal combinations), MAPO (modality-stratified batch optimization to reduce variance from mixed group advantages), and adaptive weighting/curriculum scheduling for prioritizing difficult modality combinations. This approach directly improves robustness to real-world distribution shifts where modalities may be missing, corrupted, or reweighted.

Bootstrapping Action-Grounded Visual Dynamics in Unified Vision-Language Models

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-13

💼 25 ⚡ 72

# Summary This work reveals that unified VLMs struggle with forward dynamics prediction (FDP) but excel at the inverse task—predicting actions from frame pairs—creating an exploitable asymmetry. The researchers leverage this by using learned inverse dynamics prediction (IDP) to bootstrap FDP through two mechanisms: annotating unlabeled video frames to scale training data, and using IDP as a reward signal for inference-time search, effectively converting a harder prediction problem into a more tractable supervised learning task. **Key implication**: This bootstrapping approach suggests a general principle for multimodal models—identifying and exploiting asymmetries in learning difficulty between related tasks can circumvent training challenges in physically grounded reasoning.

Bootstrapping Action-Grounded Visual Dynamics in Unified Vision-Language Models

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-13

💼 23 ⚡ 72

This paper demonstrates that unified VLMs can learn forward dynamics prediction (FDP) more effectively by first bootstrapping inverse dynamics prediction (IDP)—leveraging the asymmetry that action captioning between frames is easier than generating future frames from language instructions. The authors propose two concrete strategies: using IDP to annotate unlabeled video frames for expanded FDP training data, and employing IDP as a reward function for inference-time verification to guide FDP sample selection. This approach addresses a fundamental limitation in multimodal grounding and offers a practical pathway for scaling action-conditional video generation without extensive paired supervision.

Credit Where It is Due: Cross-Modality Connectivity Drives Precise Reinforcement Learning for MLLM Reasoning

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-13

💼 18 ⚡ 72

# Summary Researchers identified that only ~15% of tokens in MLLMs exhibit strong cross-modal attention connectivity during visual reasoning, acting as semantic anchors that ground reasoning in images while others follow linguistic patterns. They propose Anchor-Token Reinforcement Learning (AT-RL), which selectively applies RLVR to these high-connectivity tokens via graph-based attention clustering, achieving performance gains with minimal computational overhead (~1.2% increase). This work provides mechanistic insight into multimodal reasoning and offers a practical optimization for credit assignment in MLLM reinforcement learning pipelines.

Zooming without Zooming: Region-to-Image Distillation for Fine-Grained Multimodal Perception

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-13

💼 25 ⚡ 72

This paper introduces Region-to-Image Distillation, a training-time technique that distills fine-grained perception knowledge from zoomed micro-crops into MLLMs, eliminating the inference-time latency overhead of iterative zooming tools while preserving accuracy gains. The approach leverages teacher models to generate region-specific VQA supervision from high-resolution crops, then distills this region-grounded knowledge back to full-image representations, enabling single-pass fine-grained understanding. This is particularly valuable for production deployments where latency is critical, trading training compute for inference efficiency in visual reasoning tasks requiring small-detail detection.

This approach demonstrates practical value for real-time cyclone monitoring in operational meteorology where deployment on edge hardware is critical for coastal hazard prediction.

MapReduce LoRA: Advancing the Pareto Front in Multi-Preference Optimization for Generative Models

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-13

💼 22 ⚡ 62

This paper addresses multi-objective alignment in generative models by introducing MapReduce LoRA—a method that trains preference-specific LoRA experts in parallel and iteratively merges them—combined with Reward-aware Token Embedding (RaTE) for runtime preference composition, eliminating the typical alignment tax where optimizing one reward dimension degrades others. Empirical validation across text-to-image (Stable Diffusion 3.5, FLUX.1) and text-to-video (HunyuanVideo) models shows significant gains (32-67% improvements on vision metrics, 48-90% on video quality), offering a practical path for efficient multi-reward optimization without expensive retraining. The approach's modularity—training experts independently then merging—provides both computational efficiency and inference-time flexibility for weighted preference control.

Zooming without Zooming: Region-to-Image Distillation for Fine-Grained Multimodal Perception

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-13

💼 23 ⚡ 72

This paper introduces Region-to-Image Distillation, a training-time knowledge distillation approach that eliminates the inference-time latency bottleneck of iterative zooming methods by baking fine-grained perception directly into MLLMs during training—teacher models generate high-quality VQA annotations from micro-cropped regions, which are then distilled back to full-image understanding. The key innovation is converting zooming from a costly agentic tool requiring repeated visual encoding into an implicit capability, enabling single-pass fine-grained perception without sacrificing speed or requiring external tools at inference. This approach addresses a critical limitation in vision-language models where small discriminative details get lost in global context, with direct applications to document understanding, medical imaging, and other detail-dependent multimodal tasks.
