On Robustness of Vision-Language-Action Model against Multi-Modal Perturbations

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-25

💼 22 ⚡ 72

This paper systematically evaluates Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models across 17 multi-modal perturbations, revealing that actions are the most vulnerable modality while existing visual robustness techniques don't transfer to other modalities. The authors propose RobustVLA, which uses offline robust optimization with worst-case action noise adversarial training on flow matching objectives combined with input consistency enforcement to build genuinely multi-modal robust VLAs for real-world deployment. This work is significant for robotics and embodied AI practitioners, as it addresses a critical gap between lab conditions and production robustness across all input/output modalities rather than just vision.

This addresses a critical gap in video understanding by balancing task accuracy with computational efficiency—particularly valuable for deployment scenarios with restricted budgets.

This benchmark targets a critical limitation in current VLMs—their inability to reason about complex interactive dynamics in unscripted real-world scenes—making it essential for developing traffic prediction and autonomous system evaluation applications.

The framework extends to video understanding via on-demand context construction—selectively sampling task-relevant frames to reduce visual tokens while maintaining reasoning quality—enabling both stronger performance and improved computational efficiency in open-weight multimodal agents.
