cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-03

💼 18 ⚡ 72

CamReasoner reformulates camera movement classification from black-box prediction to structured geometric reasoning by implementing an Observation-Thinking-Answer paradigm that explicitly decodes spatio-temporal cues like trajectories and view frustums. This approach addresses a critical gap in multimodal video understanding by prioritizing cinematic geometry over superficial visual patterns, enabling more robust discrimination between physically distinct camera motions.

FastSLM: Hierarchical Frame Q-Former for Effective Speech Modality Adaptation

💼 18 ⚡ 78

FastSLM introduces a Hierarchical Frame Q-Former that achieves extreme temporal compression of speech inputs, reducing them to just 1.67 tokens/second—a 93% reduction—by progressively distilling acoustic features across multiple scales rather than projecting raw high-frame-rate features directly to LLM space. This token-efficient architecture specifically targets the scalability bottleneck that makes long-form speech processing computationally prohibitive in Multimodal LLMs, enabling practical deployment of speech-language models without sacrificing semantic information through aggressive compression.

PolarMem addresses a critical gap in multimodal agent memory by introducing a training-free system that converts probabilistic vision-language model outputs into verifiable logical constraints through non-parametric partitioning and polarized graph topology with explicit inhibitory connections. The key innovation is encoding negative constraints as primary cognitive states rather than treating them implicitly, enabling logic-dominant retrieval that grounds long-horizon agent reasoning in provable evidence rather than semantic affinity alone. This approach is particularly significant for safety-critical applications where distinguishing what an agent knows from what it merely perceives with high confidence is essential.

Do All Individual Layers Help? An Empirical Study of Task-Interfering Layers in Vision-Language Models

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-03

💼 22 ⚡ 72

This paper reveals that Vision-Language Models contain "task-interfering layers" that actively degrade performance on specific downstream tasks, and demonstrates that selectively zeroing parameters in these layers can improve results—suggesting current VLM architectures are sub-optimal. The authors introduce Task-Layer Interaction Vectors as a diagnostic tool to systematically identify harmful layers, with findings generalizable across models and datasets, opening new optimization avenues beyond standard fine-tuning. This work challenges the assumption that all pre-trained layers contribute positively and suggests pruning-aware training or adaptive layer selection could significantly improve VLM efficiency and performance.

💼 22 ⚡ 78

MACD introduces a model-aware contrastive decoding approach that reduces hallucinations in Video-LLMs by using the model's own attention/feedback to identify and surgically modify object-level regions that trigger false outputs, rather than relying on random perturbations. This targeted counterfactual construction enables more effective evidence-grounded token selection during inference, addressing a key weakness of existing contrastive decoding methods that lack precision in controlling which visual cues are altered. The approach has significant practical implications for deploying Video-LLMs in accuracy-critical applications where grounding to actual visual content is essential.
