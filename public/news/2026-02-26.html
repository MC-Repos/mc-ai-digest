A Hidden Semantic Bottleneck in Conditional Embeddings of Diffusion Transformers

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-26

💼 28 ⚡ 72

This work reveals a critical inefficiency in Diffusion Transformers' conditional embeddings: class and continuous conditions exhibit extreme angular similarity (>99%) with semantic information concentrated in a minority of dimensions, creating a "semantic bottleneck." The authors demonstrate that two-thirds of the embedding space can be pruned without degradation—and sometimes with improved generation quality—suggesting current diffusion models dramatically overparameterize their conditioning mechanisms. This finding opens opportunities for more efficient model architectures and provides insights into how these models encode and utilize control signals during generation.

This addresses a critical bottleneck in diffusion model deployment—current scaling methods suffer from quality degradation and sublinear speedup, making this conditional guidance scheduling approach particularly relevant for production systems requiring both speed and fidelity.
