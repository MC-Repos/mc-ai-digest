CamReasoner: Reinforcing Camera Movement Understanding via Structured Spatial Reasoning

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-12

💼 28 ⚡ 75

CamReasoner reformulates camera motion understanding from black-box classification to structured reasoning by requiring models to explicitly decode spatio-temporal cues (trajectories, view frustums) through an Observation-Thinking-Answer paradigm, addressing confusion between physically distinct motions. The framework is trained on 18k supervised reasoning chains and 38k RL feedback samples—notably the first application of reinforcement learning for logical alignment in camera understanding, grounding predictions in geometric principles rather than superficial visual patterns. This approach bridges multimodal perception with cinematic logic, enabling more robust video spatial intelligence for applications requiring precise camera dynamics interpretation.

SoulX-FlashHead: Oracle-guided Generation of Infinite Real-time Streaming Talking Heads

cs.CV updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-12

💼 35 ⚡ 78

SoulX-FlashHead presents a 1.3B-parameter model addressing the critical challenge of real-time streaming talking head generation by introducing Streaming-Aware Spatiotemporal Pre-training with Temporal Audio Context Cache to stabilize audio feature extraction from short fragments, and Oracle-Guided Bidirectional Distillation to prevent error accumulation and identity drift in long-sequence generation. This approach achieves a practical balance between computational efficiency and visual fidelity, enabling infinite-length streaming without the prohibitive costs of large-scale models or quality degradation of lightweight alternatives. The work's focus on streaming-specific challenges—temporal audio coherence and autoregressive error propagation—represents meaningful progress toward production-ready audio-driven video synthesis for applications like live telepresence and real-time content generation.

This testbed bridges the gap between coding agent evaluation and multimodal AI capabilities, providing researchers with a realistic, high-complexity domain for advancing agentic systems that require both deep code understanding and visual-spatial reasoning.

Safety Recovery in Reasoning Models Is Only a Few Early Steering Steps Away

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-12

💼 28 ⚡ 35

SafeThink addresses a critical safety degradation problem in RL-optimized reasoning models (like GRPO) by introducing a lightweight inference-time defense that monitors reasoning traces with a safety reward model and surgically injects corrective prefixes only when safety thresholds are breached. This satisficing-based approach (rather than maximizing safety) achieves 30-60% attack success rate reduction across multiple MLRMs and jailbreak benchmarks while maintaining reasoning performance—offering a practical post-hoc solution that doesn't require retraining. The method's elegance lies in treating safety as a constraint that guides reasoning rather than competing with it, suggesting a more nuanced approach to aligning models trained with reasoning-optimized RL objectives.

Beyond VLM-Based Rewards: Diffusion-Native Latent Reward Modeling

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-02-12

💼 18 ⚡ 45

DiNa-LRM proposes a diffusion-native latent reward model that eliminates the pixel-space domain mismatch of VLM-based rewards by directly operating on noisy diffusion latents with noise-calibrated uncertainty estimation. The method leverages a pretrained latent diffusion backbone with a timestep-conditioned reward head and inference-time noise ensembling, offering computational efficiency gains and improved alignment for preference optimization in diffusion/flow-matching models. This approach enables test-time scaling without external multimodal priors, addressing both the memory overhead and domain alignment limitations of traditional VLM-based reward functions.
