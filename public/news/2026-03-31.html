Adversarial Attacks on Multimodal Large Language Models: A Comprehensive Survey

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-03-31

💼 22 ⚡ 78

This survey systematizes adversarial vulnerabilities in MLLMs by moving beyond cataloging attacks to identify shared architectural weaknesses across modalities—organizing threats through attacker objectives and vulnerability patterns rather than attack type alone. The work reveals that multimodal integration creates amplified attack surfaces compared to unimodal models, with integrity attacks, jailbreaks, instruction hijacking, and poisoning rooted in common representational failures in how these models fuse cross-modal information. For practitioners, this taxonomy provides a framework for understanding that defenses must address fundamental fusion mechanisms rather than treating modality-specific threats in isolation.

Hydra: Unifying Document Retrieval and Generation in a Single Vision-Language Model

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-03-31

💼 28 ⚡ 72

Hydra unifies document retrieval and generation in a single VLM by toggling a LoRA adapter at inference time—enabling ColBERT-style late-interaction embeddings for retrieval while preserving byte-identical generation quality through careful engineering (attention restoration, lm_head preservation, KV-cache-aware decoding). This approach halves the memory and system complexity of traditional dual-model pipelines while maintaining retrieval performance and demonstrating negligible generation degradation (max delta-ANLS = 0.0044) across VQA benchmarks. The work reveals critical engineering subtleties for parameter-efficient adaptation that prevents silent generation failures—valuable insights for practitioners building efficient multimodal systems.

eess.AS updates on arXiv.org · 2026-03-31

💼 28 ⚡ 87

This work proposes a hierarchical cross-modal fusion network to predict human perception of AI-dubbed content by integrating audio (speaker identity, prosody), video (facial expressions, scene context), and text (semantic information) through progressive intra and inter-modal fusion layers. Diagnosing and Repairing Unsafe Channels in Vision-Language Models via Causal Discovery and Dual-Modal Safety Subspace Projection

cs.AI updates on arXiv.org · 2026-03-31

💼 23 ⚡ 72

This paper presents CARE, a framework that uses causal mediation analysis to identify neurons responsible for unsafe outputs in Vision-Language Models, then repairs them via dual-modal safety subspace projection—learning generalized safety subspaces from benign/malicious activation pairs and dynamically projecting activations toward these spaces during inference. The approach combines causal interpretability with modal-specific safety constraints through adaptive fusion, offering a mechanistic alternative to traditional fine-tuning for mitigating jailbreaks while maintaining semantic performance. This represents a significant advancement in interpretable safety for multimodal systems, moving beyond black-box guardrails toward surgically targeted intervention on unsafe computational pathways.
