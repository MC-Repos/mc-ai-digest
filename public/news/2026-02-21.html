NVIDIA's DreamDojo is an open-source world model that learns robot dynamics directly from 44,711 hours of real-world human video data, replacing traditional physics engines with learned pixel-space prediction of robot actions. This approach eliminates the manual coding and 3D modeling overhead of conventional simulators while enabling better generalization to diverse real-world scenarios. The model represents a significant shift toward data-driven simulation for robotics, potentially accelerating robot learning and reducing sim-to-real transfer gaps.
